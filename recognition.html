Awards & Honours

Awards & Fellowships

Prestigious recognitions from national academies, government agencies, and international funding bodies.

2022

DST-INSPIRE Faculty Award

Department of Science and Technology, Government of India

Competitive faculty fellowship awarded to early-career scientists with outstanding research potential. Supports independent research on oral microbiome signatures in cancer for a period of five years with research grant including consumables, equipment, and fellowship support.

2020

ICMR Young Scientist Award

Indian Council of Medical Research

National award recognizing exceptional contributions by young biomedical researchers in India. Awarded for pioneering work on metagenomic characterization of the oral microbiome in oral squamous cell carcinoma and its translational implications for early detection.

2021

NASI-Young Scientist Award

National Academy of Sciences, India

Prestigious award conferred by the National Academy of Sciences, India to young scientists demonstrating exceptional research contributions in life sciences. Recognized for studies on microbial dysbiosis in oral carcinogenesis and the development of diagnostic biomarkers.

2023

Ramanujan Fellowship

Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB), DST

Highly competitive fellowship for outstanding scientists and engineers to pursue research in Indian institutions. Supports the establishment of an independent research programme in cancer genomics and spatial transcriptomics at ICMR-NICPR.

2024

Wellcome Trust/DBT India Alliance Fellowship

Wellcome Trust / Department of Biotechnology, India Alliance

Intermediate Fellowship in Biomedical Research providing comprehensive support for a five-year research programme on spatial transcriptomics of the tumor microenvironment in oral cancer, including salary, research costs, and collaborative travel.

2023

Best Paper Award

International Conference on Microbiome Research, New Delhi

Awarded for the presented research on "Multi-omics integration reveals microbial drivers of oral carcinogenesis," recognized by an international panel for scientific rigour, novelty, and translational potential in microbiome-based cancer diagnostics.
